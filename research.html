Research

Minerals, magmas, textures

My work focuses on how mafic-ultramafic and island-arc systems evolve, how metals move through melts and fluids, and how mineral assemblages preserve that history from the outcrop scale to the nanoscale.

Themes

Research areas

Layered intrusions

Studying the Bushveld and other layered mafic-ultramafic systems to understand petrogenesis, stratigraphy, chromitite formation, and metal distribution in Ni-Cu-PGE-bearing environments.

Chromitites

Investigating how chromitites form through magmatic processes, assimilation, melt-rock interaction, and later fluid overprints.

PGE mobility

Following platinum-group elements and minerals across alloys, oxides, inclusions, and alteration assemblages to constrain metal cycling in melts and fluids.

Melt-rock interaction

Using petrography, mineral chemistry, and multiphase inclusions to reconstruct how magmatic and hydrothermal processes modify ultramafic rocks.

Early Earth isotopes

Applying triple oxygen and hydrogen isotopes to altered metalavas and related rocks to explore hydrothermal exchange, seawater interaction, and ancient surface conditions.

Arc magmas

Examining primitive high-K arc magmas, Ural-Alaskan type complexes, and the relationships between volcanic and plutonic suites in subduction-related environments.

Workflow

How I work

The research combines field observation, petrography, microanalysis, geochemistry, isotope work, and numerical modelling. I am most interested in the points where these approaches intersect: where a thin-section texture changes the interpretation of a bulk-rock trend, or where a mineral inclusion reframes a whole model of metal mobility.

Hands-on methods

SEM-EDS, Raman spectroscopy, field portable XRF, clean-lab chemistry, and mass spectrometry.

Interpretation

LA-ICP-MS, EBSD, XRD, synchrotron micro-XRF/XAS, FIB-TEM, EPMA, and atom-probe-informed mineralogical reasoning.

Modelling

Python workflows, MAGEMin and MELTS approaches, isotope fractionation models, and mass-balance or mixing calculations.

Current questions

PGE concentration

Especially across the Bushveld Critical Zone, where the balance between magmatic and post-magmatic processes remains a central problem.

Low-T PGE behaviour

Silicate-oxide-platinum-group mineral intergrowths offer windows into processes that are still poorly constrained.

Isotope signals

Oxygen and hydrogen isotopes provide powerful tests of hydrothermal exchange and crustal signatures in ancient rocks.
