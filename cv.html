General information

Anton Kutyrev

Petrologist and mineralogist working on mafic-ultramafic systems, chromitites, platinum-group minerals, arc magmas, and isotope geochemistry. He is currently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the School of Geosciences at the University of the Witwatersrand, Johannesburg.

Education

  • 2020

    PhD in geology and mineral sciences, awarded after the 2019 defence through the Dissertation Council at the V.S. Sobolev Institute of Geology and Mineralogy. Research was conducted at the Institute of Volcanology and Seismology in Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky.

  • 2017

    Specialist degree with distinction in applied geology, Saint Petersburg Mining University. Five-year full-time degree broadly equivalent to an MSc.

Research focus

Petrology and mineralogy of mafic-ultramafic and island-arc systems; melt-rock and fluid-rock interaction; origin of chromitites; PGE mineralogy from hand sample to nanoscale, including alloys and PGE oxides; PGE and PGM cycling and mobility in melts and fluids in ultramafic rocks and layered intrusions; oxygen isotopes as tracers of crustal recycling and early Earth surface environments; and critical minerals in the context of the energy transition.

Analytical and modelling skills

  • Hands-on: SEM-EDS, Raman spectroscopy, field-portable XRF, clean-lab chemistry, and mass spectrometry for Rb-Sr, Sm-Nd, and triple-oxygen isotopes using laser fluorination.

  • Data reduction and interpretation: LA-ICP-MS, EBSD, X-ray diffraction, synchrotron micro-XRF/XAS, FIB-TEM, EPMA, and atom-probe tomography.

  • Modelling and data processing: Python and spreadsheet-based workflows, MELTS and MAGEMin thermodynamic modelling, Monte Carlo modelling of triple-oxygen isotope exchange, and mass-balance and mixing models.
